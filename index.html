The 1970 Shift

Before York enacted its comprehensive zoning framework in 1970, neighborhoods were built organically around walkability. High lot coverages, zero-lot lines, and mixed corner retail predominated. Modern code effectively made re-building this historic identity illegal.

Why Parcels Disappear Below

The Premium Paradox

Because modern zoning bans rowhouses and high density, these historic forms are frozen in artificial scarcity. Our market analysis shows buyers pay an implicit price premium to live in these "illegal" layouts due to constrained supply.
